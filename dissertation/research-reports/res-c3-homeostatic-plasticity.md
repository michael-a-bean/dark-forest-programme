ANALYSIS: The literature reveals a multi-scale regulatory architecture where functional diversity is not merely preserved but actively generated by heterogeneous local homeostatic mechanisms. Four key threads emerged:

(1) **Turrigiano's synaptic scaling** preserves relative weight differences through multiplicative adjustment, and her 2025 PNAS paper (Wen et al.) demonstrates that synaptic and intrinsic homeostatic plasticity operate as a *modular system* -- independently recruited, regulating distinct network features. Wu et al. (2020 PNAS) computationally confirmed that synaptic scaling restores correlation structure while intrinsic plasticity restores firing rates -- non-overlapping roles.

(2) **BCM sliding threshold** as metaplasticity is well-established from Abraham & Bear (1996) through Abraham (2008 Nature Reviews Neuroscience). The modification threshold dynamically adjusts based on activity history, making LTP harder after high activity and easier after low activity.

(3) **Hebbian-homeostatic interaction** produces heterogeneity because these forms are inversely correlated at the individual level (Prosper et al. 2025, J Physiol) and cell-type-specific (Lambo & Bhatt 2022 -- RS vs. IB neurons use different molecular pathways). Eckmann et al. (2024 PNAS) showed synapse-type-specific competition forms functional recurrent networks without global homeostasis. Zenke et al. (2017) identified the temporal paradox: slow homeostatic plasticity cannot stabilize fast Hebbian learning, necessitating metaplastic mechanisms at intermediate timescales.

(4) **Local homeostatic regulation** produces functional specialization: Liu, Seay & Buonomano (2023 J Neurosci) showed that differential activation creates distinct ensembles through cell-specific homeostatic adjustments. The Feighan et al. (2026) review reveals molecular convergence/divergence between Hebbian and homeostatic pathways, explaining how the same synapse can switch plasticity modes.

(5) **Metaplasticity and catastrophic forgetting**: Jedlicka et al. (2022 Trends Neurosci) is the key review connecting biological metaplasticity to computational continual learning. Laborieux et al. (2021 Nat Commun) showed metaplastic variables in binarized networks prevent forgetting by creating a stable-to-flexible synapse spectrum. Chen et al. (2023 Sci Adv) demonstrated that metaplasticity-based neuromodulation preserves synaptic diversity by preventing extreme weight changes.
