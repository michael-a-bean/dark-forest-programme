# Sharing the Dark Forest Programme

## The pitch (one paragraph)

A late-night conversation about Fermi's paradox and Deleuze turned into a
five-paper research programme in twelve days. The computation is real
(15,000 simulation jobs, 4.6M observations, physical Ray cluster). The
academic process (committee, peer review, defence) was simulated using AI
agents. The meta-paper documents the whole thing — including the part where
a literature review found that half the findings had already been published
by other people. The repo contains everything: code, data, papers, committee
transcripts, audit reports, and the honest reckoning with what went right
and wrong.

## What to expect

People will focus on:
1. The speed (12 days) — some impressed, some dismissive ("toy system")
2. The prior art (50% convergence) — some see failure, some see validation
3. The institutional implications — Crawford's question about PhD programmes
4. The meta-paper itself — whether documenting a simulated process has value
5. Whether the science is real — Mitchell's question about metaphor vs mechanism

The honest response to all of these is in the repo. Don't oversell. Let people read and form their own view.
