## Associate Editor Assessment

All Round 2 concerns verified as resolved. The paper makes a genuine contribution through:

1. Systematic characterization of three-regime structure in optimization-free Hebbian networks, replicated across two architectures
2. The passive mirror finding: networks reflect instantaneous co-activation density, not temporal correlation (shuffled-text dissociation)
3. Spontaneous individuation from identical substrate, partially overturning the passive mirror and establishing tracing→mapping transition
4. Instability of multiplicity constraining expectations for Hebbian self-organized ecologies
5. Generative (not decorative) use of Deleuzian framework in experimental design

## Noted Limitations (not requiring revision)

- Small network scale (64/120/400 nodes)
- Reliance on text as primary structured input
- Philosophical framework difficult for empirical reviewers to evaluate independently
- All acknowledged by authors

## Commendation

"The combination of systematic empirical characterization with a generative (if unconventional) philosophical framework is distinctive and well-suited to this journal's interdisciplinary mission."
