# Artificial Life — Editorial Decision (R2)

**Manuscript:** "Coexistence Without Differentiation: The Self-Sealing Dynamics of Hebbian Individuation"

## Decision: ACCEPT

Dear Michael Bean,

Thank you for your thorough revision. All three reviewers are satisfied that the major concerns from R1 have been addressed. The new edge injection experiment with controlled initialisation weights is the standout addition — the discovery of "functional absorption" (moderate-weight edges trigger merger rather than differentiation) and the "self-sealing dilemma" (edges strong enough to carry signal are absorbed; edges too weak carry no signal) substantially strengthen the paper's central claim.

Reviewer 2, who recommended Major Revision at R1, now recommends Accept with Minor Revision, noting that the edge injection results are "more interesting than expected" and constitute "a genuine mechanistic insight." Reviewer 3 describes the philosophical integration of bare vs. complex repetition as "no longer decorative" and the individuation/differenciation diagnostic as "the paper's strongest philosophical contribution." Reviewer 1 notes the revision addresses all major issues and most minor ones.

I am pleased to accept this manuscript for publication in *Artificial Life*, subject to the following minor editorial corrections that do not require re-review:

### Minor Corrections (no re-review needed)

1. **Assemblage detection threshold sensitivity** (R2, m1): The 210-run sensitivity analysis is referenced but not shown. Add a brief inline summary or supplementary table.

2. **Decay rate vs. diagram claim** (R2): One sentence acknowledging that coexistence at a single λ value may represent a critical point rather than a topological invariant.

3. **Coupling similarity time-course** (R1, N3): If data exists, showing whether tight-locality differentiation is present from session 1 or develops gradually would strengthen the narrative. Optional.

### Summary

The three-paper arc — from passive mirroring (Paper 1) through spatial individuation (Paper 2) to the structural impossibility of differentiation (Paper 3) — provides a compelling characterisation of the limits of correlation-based learning in symmetric substrates. The self-sealing concept, grounded in both analytic argument and comprehensive empirical verification across 1,400 simulations, names a phenomenon not previously articulated in the literature. The dual mechanism (topological isolation + functional absorption) is a genuine contribution to our understanding of what Hebbian substrates can and cannot achieve.

Congratulations on a fine piece of work.

Sincerely,
*Editor, Artificial Life*
