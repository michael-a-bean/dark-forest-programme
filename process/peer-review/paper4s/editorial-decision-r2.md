# Artificial Life — Editorial Decision (Round 2)

**Manuscript:** "Pruning as Confound: How Maintenance Processes Obscure Functional Diversity in Self-Organising Networks"
**Authors:** Michael Bean
**Date:** March 24, 2026

---

## Decision: ACCEPT

Dear Michael Bean,

Thank you for your thorough revision and the substantial additional experiments. The three reviewers are now in agreement that the manuscript meets the standard for publication.

Reviewer 2, who raised the most critical concern in Round 1 (the edge cap confound), now writes: *"The authors have responded substantively to every concern. The edge cap sweep converts what was a potential fatal confound into a positive empirical result."* Recommendation: **Accept.**

Reviewer 1 notes one minor discrepancy between the response letter's claim that the primary rank number was updated to 5.6 and certain references in the companion Paper 4 manuscript that retained 4.7. This is a cross-paper consistency issue rather than an error in Paper 4S itself. Recommendation: **Minor Revision** (textual only).

Reviewer 3 identified that the terminological corrections ("limit condition" replacing "structural germ") were implemented in Paper 4S but not in the companion Paper 4, which Reviewer 3 also consulted. Again, a cross-paper issue. Recommendation: **Minor Revision** (textual only).

I am satisfied that Paper 4S itself addresses all required and recommended revisions. The cross-paper consistency issues identified by R1 and R3 pertain to the companion manuscript (Paper 4), not to the manuscript under review. I recommend the authors ensure consistency across the paper series but do not require this as a condition of acceptance for Paper 4S.

The paper makes a genuine methodological contribution to the computational artificial life community. The identification of the pruning confound, its characterisation through the disordinal interaction and threshold sweep, the grid-size scaling law, the CCD null model invalidation, and the response rank validation with participation ratio collectively represent a thorough and honest reckoning with a programme's own assumptions. The philosophical framework, now corrected to use Simondon's "limit condition" rather than "structural germ," is deployed with precision.

We are pleased to accept this manuscript for publication in *Artificial Life*.

Sincerely,
*Editor, Artificial Life*
